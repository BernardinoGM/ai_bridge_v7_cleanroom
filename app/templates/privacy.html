Privacy Policy

AI Bridge processes account data, routed usage records, payment records, referral attribution, security events, and de-identified operational analytics so the service can run safely, bill correctly, improve routing outcomes, and protect the platform. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, what operational rights we retain, and what choices users have.

Sections Collection Use of information Prompt and content handling Telemetry and analytics Sharing Retention and security User choices

Information we collect

We collect account identifiers such as name, email address, referral code, session identifiers, and issued API keys. We also collect billing and ledger records such as top-ups, starter credits, bonus credits, rewards, webhook events, and payment state so the service can maintain a correct internal balance.

AI Bridge also records operational usage data such as endpoint used, mode selected, routing outcome, latency, spend estimates, failure events, and task continuity records. These records help preserve multi-turn work, support account recovery, investigate payment failures, and keep routing stable.

How we use information

We use collected information to provide routed model access, issue keys, maintain balances, attribute referrals, process payments, preserve continuity across sessions, detect abuse, investigate failures, and maintain an accurate ledger of credits and debits.

We also use operational information to monitor service quality, evaluate routing outcomes, identify repeated workload patterns, improve spend control, and make product and operational decisions. This includes derived usage patterns, routing outcomes, and de-identified analytics needed to improve reliability and protect the platform.

Prompt and content handling

Public landing demos avoid prompt storage beyond limited anonymous trial accounting and internal subsidy inspection. Signed-in product flows may retain prompt excerpts, response excerpts, task state, request payloads, and derived routing outcomes where reasonably necessary for continuity, billing correctness, support, safety review, service improvement, or abuse prevention.

AI Bridge does not publicly promise unrestricted training on raw user content. When we use product data for service improvement, we focus on operational telemetry, routing outcomes, repeated workload patterns, de-identified analytics, and abuse-prevention signals rather than a blanket right to train on raw prompt text without limit.

Telemetry, routing outcomes, and de-identified analytics

AI Bridge retains broad lawful rights to use telemetry, routing outcomes, derived usage patterns, benchmark comparisons, spend trends, failure signals, and de-identified analytics for service protection, abuse prevention, compliance, service improvement, product planning, and operational monitoring. These rights are necessary to run a routing product safely and commercially.

Where reasonably possible, analytics and product learning use aggregated or de-identified records. Some user-linked operational data must still be retained for billing correctness, fraud investigation, support, and legal compliance.

When we share information

We may share necessary records with payment processors, infrastructure providers, model providers, fraud-prevention vendors, professional advisors, and legal authorities where reasonably required to operate the service, investigate abuse, comply with law, protect our rights, or respond to security incidents.

We do not sell personal information in the ordinary consumer-ad-tech sense described by some privacy regimes. Operational sharing is limited to what is reasonably necessary to provide and protect the service.

Retention and security

We retain records for as long as reasonably necessary to provide the service, resolve disputes, maintain accurate billing and ledger history, investigate abuse, and comply with legal obligations. Retention periods may differ depending on the type of data involved, including payment records, fraud signals, request failures, and continuity records.

No service is perfectly secure, but AI Bridge uses layered controls appropriate for a commercial launch product, including session controls, internal access limitations, payment verification, and failure logging.

Your choices and contact

You may contact us to request account assistance, key rotation, or account closure. Some operational records, ledger entries, payment records, and security logs may be retained where legally required or operationally necessary.

Questions about this Privacy Policy can be sent to hello@getaibridge.com.
